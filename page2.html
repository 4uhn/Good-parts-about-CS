Computer Science in Medicine

James Harvey October 27 , 2023

Technology is being used all throughout the medical field in todays age, and computer science plays a very large role in helping innovate and maintain this technology. Breakthroughs in AI and computer vision software have allowed Doctors to be able to produce more reliable diagnosis and a computer's ability to store and process large amounts of data has allowed medical records to have a solely online prescense.


Data analysis is a large part of computer science and the medical field as well, a lot of data is stored for each individual patient and it is the job of computer scientists to make sure that this data is easily accessible, stored safely and private. Any breaches in security could have devastating impacts on both patients and hospital workers.

CT scanners are large scanners which use x-rays and computers to build a vision of the internals in the body, without the brain of the computer the only data produced by the sensors would be the time taken for x-rays to reach the other side. An algorithm is required to turn these numbers into a visual image that doctors can use to diagnose and potentially prescribe further treatment.


Medical imaging technology is only improving and with new development in AI diagnosis is becoming increasingly easier. These AI harness amchine learning algorthims which are trained using vast amounts of validated data from past diagnosises, then it uses its training to attempt to spot patterns in data it is given and using these patters to output a decision
