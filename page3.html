Dia

The Ukrainian e-governance breakthrough

Alex Balats October 230 , 2023

The creation of Dia, a state-owned e-governance platform, is a major step towards the digitalization of Ukraine. It has kept the country running during wartime, as well as provided a myriad of other benefits for the people and government of Ukraine.

It was officially launched on the 6th of Feb in 2020, as a work of 25 ukrainians who were set on transforming the country. Mykhailo Fedorov the deputy prime minister told how the app was approached:

like a “start up” but without the profit motive

- Mykhailo Fedorov, Minister of digital transformation

This resulted in ukrainians having access to 117 services, including things like management and use of all official government papers, payment of taxes and bills, and even the ability to register a business. Initially the app was made to help out during the covid pandemic but eventually ended up being a pillar of the ukrainian government, especially during the time of the Russian Invasion

Importance

The first benefit of this app that comes to mind is the ability to access and use all of your official document online, in turn helping with decreasing beurocracy associated with public services, helping with corruption and cutting government costs along the way. Fewer people are needed to be employed in the public sector and fewer human interractions happen due to the access to all of the services. This also help with the efficiency, speed and transparency of all the services on offer.
